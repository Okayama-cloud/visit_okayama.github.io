Welcome to Okayama

Located in western Japan, Okayama Prefecture offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, historical treasures, and modern delights

Nestled between the Seto Inland Sea and the ChÅ«goku Mountains, Okayama is a destination that combines Japan's timeless beauty with authentic local experiences. Known for its mild climate, the region boasts some of Japan's most significant cultural treasures while remaining refreshingly uncrowded.

From the iconic "Crow Castle" and the picture-perfect Korakuen Garden to the historic merchant district of Kurashiki and the sun-drenched islands of the Seto Inland Sea, Okayama offers unforgettable memories for every type of traveler.

Top Attractions

Must-visit landmarks and destinations that showcase Okayama's unique blend of nature and culture

Okayama Castle

Known as "Crow Castle" for its striking black exterior, this magnificent structure dates back to 1597. The castle offers panoramic views of the city and houses fascinating exhibits on samurai culture.

Learn More

Korakuen Garden

One of Japan's three most beautiful landscape gardens, Korakuen features meticulously manicured lawns, peaceful ponds, traditional tea houses, and stunning seasonal displays throughout the year.

Learn More

Kurashiki Bikan District

Step back in time in this beautifully preserved merchant district, with its willow-lined canals, white-walled storehouses, and boutique shops selling local crafts and specialties.

Learn More

Kibitsu Shrine

This ancient Shinto shrine features one of Japan's longest wooden corridors and is associated with the legend of Momotaro, a folk hero born from a giant peach who defeated ogres on Ogre Island.

Unique Experiences

Immerse yourself in authentic Okayama activities and create unforgettable memories

Peach Picking

Enjoy Okayama's famous white peaches straight from the orchard during summer. These juicy, sweet fruits are known as "The Queen of Fruits" throughout Japan for their exceptional quality.

Learn More

Bizen Pottery Workshop

Create your own traditional Bizen ware pottery, one of Japan's oldest pottery styles known for its earthy, rustic aesthetic and unique firing technique that creates natural ash glazes.

Learn More

Local Food Tour

Taste Okayama's specialties like Barazushi (scattered sushi), Demi-Katsu Don (pork cutlet with demi-glace sauce), and fresh seafood from the Seto Inland Sea on a guided culinary adventure.

Learn More

Inland Sea Cycling

Cycle across the breathtaking Seto Inland Sea on dedicated bike paths connecting picturesque islands, with stunning ocean views, art installations, and charming fishing villages.

Seasonal Highlights

Okayama offers unique experiences throughout the year, each season bringing its own special charm

Spring

Cherry Blossom Magic

Experience the breathtaking cherry blossoms at Korakuen Garden and along the Asahi River. The annual Cherry Blossom Festival features illuminated trees, traditional performances, and food stalls.

Summer

Fruit Paradise

Summer brings Okayama's famous white peaches and muscat grapes. Visit fruit farms for picking experiences, enjoy water activities on the Seto Inland Sea, and join vibrant local festivals with fireworks.

Autumn

Colorful Foliage

Autumn transforms Okayama's landscapes with brilliant red and gold foliage. Hiking in the Kibi Plain or visiting mountain temples offers spectacular views, along with seasonal harvests and cultural festivals.

Winter

Winter Wonders

Winter brings a peaceful atmosphere to Okayama's attractions. Enjoy the illuminated Okayama Castle, winter illuminations at Korakuen Garden, and the unique opportunity to see snow-covered historical sites without the crowds.

Travel Tips

Essential information to help you plan your perfect Okayama adventure

Getting There

  • Okayama is serviced by Okayama Airport with connections to major Japanese cities
  • The Shinkansen (bullet train) connects Okayama to Tokyo (3.5 hours) and Osaka (45 minutes)
  • JR trains provide access to nearby attractions and cities

Where to Stay

  • Downtown Okayama offers modern hotels near the train station
  • Kurashiki provides traditional ryokan (Japanese inns) for an authentic experience
  • Rural areas feature farm stays and boutique accommodations

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Cherry blossoms and mild weather
  • Summer (June-August): Fruit picking and festivals
  • Autumn (September-November): Colorful foliage and pleasant temperatures
  • Winter (December-February): Fewer crowds and winter illuminations

Getting Around

  • JR train and local bus networks connect major attractions
  • The Okayama Electric Tramway provides easy city navigation
  • Rental bicycles are available for exploring flat areas like the Kibi Plain
  • Rental cars are recommended for reaching rural areas
